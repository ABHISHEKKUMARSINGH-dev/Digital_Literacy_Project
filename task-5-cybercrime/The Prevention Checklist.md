# Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

1. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money:** Remember that your PIN is exclusively for sending funds, never for receiving them.
2. **Verify receiver details:** Always double-check the UPI ID (VPA) and the registered name before authorizing any payment for food, transport, or fees.
3. **Beware of urgent requests:** Scammers create a false sense of urgency (e.g., "claim refund in 10 minutes"); take a moment to verify claims through official college channels.
4. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for banking:** Never conduct financial transactions or check bank balances while connected to unsecured campus or cafe Wi-Fi networks.
5. **Secure your devices:** Use biometric locks (fingerprint or face ID) specifically for your payment and banking applications to prevent unauthorized physical access.
6. **Keep OTPs private:** Never share One-Time Passwords with anyone, even if they claim to be calling from your bank or university administration.
7. **Spot phishing SMS:** Ignore unsolicited text messages offering lucrative part-time jobs or unexpected lottery winnings, as these often contain malicious links.
8. **Report immediately:** If you suspect fraud, immediately report it through the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and call the Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930.
