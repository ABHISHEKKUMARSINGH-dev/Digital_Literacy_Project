# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

### The Do's:
1. **Do curate a professional presence:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your academic projects, hackathon participation, and technical skills.
2. **Do utilize privacy settings:** Regularly audit your account settings to control who can see your personal posts and information.
3. **Do pause before posting:** Assume that anything you post online is permanent and can be seen by future employers or university staff.
4. **Do verify information:** Fact-check news stories or sensational claims before sharing them to avoid spreading misinformation.
5. **Do engage respectfully:** Disagree constructively in online discussions and maintain a polite tone, even in informal comment sections.

### The Don'ts:
1. **Don't overshare personal details:** Avoid posting your exact location, phone number, or student ID where public strangers can access it.
2. **Don't post unprofessional content:** Never share content that involves illegal activities, hate speech, or excessive partying.
3. **Don't engage with trolls:** Avoid getting dragged into heated, emotional arguments or cyberbullying in public forums.
4. **Don't plagiarize:** Never copy someone else's digital content, art, or code and claim it as your own without proper credit.
5. **Don't complain about your university/professors publicly:** Venting about grades or faculty on a public feed leaves a highly unprofessional digital footprint.
